# Meta Advertising Standards Reference

**Last verified:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards, facebook.com/policies/ads

This is a curated reference for LLM-powered ad copy and creative QA checks.
Not exhaustive — covers rules most likely to cause ad rejection or account restriction.

---

## Prohibited Content

**RULE:** No tobacco products — ads must not promote cigarettes, e-cigarettes, vapes, tobacco, or related paraphernalia.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No drugs or drug paraphernalia — ads must not promote illegal drugs, recreational drugs, or drug accessories.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection; repeated = ad account disabled
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No unsafe supplements — ads must not promote anabolic steroids, ephedra, HGH, or other products deemed unsafe.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No weapons, ammunition, or explosives — ads must not promote the sale of firearms, ammunition, paintball guns, BB guns, fireworks, or weapons of any kind.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No surveillance equipment — ads must not promote spy cameras, phone trackers, or other surveillance products marketed for spying on people.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No payday loans or predatory financial products — ads for short-term, high-interest loans (APR > 36%) are prohibited.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No counterfeit goods — ads must not promote imitation or replica products that use another brand's trademark.
**Consequence:** Ad account disabled
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** No discriminatory practices — ads must not discriminate based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, family status, disability, or medical condition.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection; repeated = account restriction
**Applies to:** all

## Restricted Content

**RULE:** Alcohol ads must not target users under the legal drinking age in the target market. Must comply with local laws and industry standards.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection in non-compliant regions
**Applies to:** copy, image, video

**RULE:** Dating service ads must be pre-approved by Meta. Must not promote infidelity or transactional relationships.
**Consequence:** Requires advertiser verification
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** Online gambling and gaming ads require prior written permission and must target approved jurisdictions only.
**Consequence:** Requires advertiser verification
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** Cryptocurrency ads must be pre-approved. Exchanges and trading platforms require regulatory licensing documentation.
**Consequence:** Requires advertiser verification
**Applies to:** all

**RULE:** Cosmetic procedures and weight loss ads have additional restrictions (see Content-Specific Rules below).
**Consequence:** Ad rejection for violations
**Applies to:** copy, image, video

**RULE:** Financial products and services ads must include all required disclosures (interest rates, fees, risks). No misleading profit claims.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** copy, landing page

## Content-Specific Rules

**RULE:** No before/after images for health, weight loss, or cosmetic procedures — ads must not show before/after comparisons that imply guaranteed or typical results for body transformation. The ban extends to "implied transformations": side-by-side comparisons, dated photo sequences, product photos next to fit/healthy person, lighting/posture changes that imply transformation, testimonial videos showing "journey" narratives. Meta's automated detection identifies these visual patterns.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** image, video

**RULE:** No personal attributes — ads must not assert or imply that the viewer has a specific personal attribute. This is the #1 rejection category (24% of all Meta rejections in 2026). Prohibited patterns: direct questions ("Are you overweight?", "Struggling with debt?"), indirect implication ("For people dealing with financial challenges"), conditional phrasing ("If you've been diagnosed with..."), empathy hooks ("We understand your pain"). Protected attributes: race, ethnicity, religion, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, family/financial status, disability, medical condition. Compliant alternatives: "Explore financial planning tools" / "Mental wellness resources available."
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** copy

**RULE:** No sensational or shocking content — ads must not use exaggerated or graphic language/imagery designed to elicit shock, fear, or outrage to drive clicks.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** copy, image, video

**RULE:** No misleading claims — ads must not make claims that are demonstrably false or create unrealistic expectations. "Lose 10kg in 1 week" = prohibited.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** copy

**RULE:** No low-quality or disruptive content — ads must not contain excessive text, clickbait tactics, engagement bait ("Tag a friend who..."), or intentionally cropped images to drive curiosity clicks.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection or reduced delivery
**Applies to:** copy, image, video

## Visual Rules

**RULE:** No misleading buttons or UI elements — images/videos must not contain fake play buttons, fake notification badges, fake close buttons, or other non-functional interactive elements.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** image, video

**RULE:** No implied platform endorsement — ads must not use the Facebook, Instagram, or Meta logos, colour schemes, or UI elements in a way that implies endorsement or partnership.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** image, video

**RULE:** No non-functional elements — images must not contain elements designed to mimic operating system or browser notifications, warning dialogs, or error messages.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** image

**RULE:** No QR codes in video ads — Meta may reject video ads that prominently feature QR codes.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** video

**RULE:** Text-heavy images — while the old "20% text rule" is no longer a hard rejection, images with excessive text overlays receive significantly less delivery. Keep text minimal for best results.
**Consequence:** Reduced delivery (soft penalty)
**Applies to:** image

**RULE:** No misleading thumbnails — video ad thumbnails must accurately represent the video content. No bait-and-switch.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** video

## Special Ad Categories

**RULE:** Housing, employment, and credit (HEC) ads must be declared as Special Ad Categories. These ads have restricted targeting — cannot target by age, gender, zip code, or many interest categories.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection; undeclared HEC ads = account restriction
**Applies to:** all (targeting + copy)

**RULE:** Social issues, elections, or politics ads require identity verification and "Paid for by" disclaimers. Subject to additional review.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection without verification
**Applies to:** all

## Landing Page Rules

**RULE:** Landing page must be functional — no broken pages, error screens, or pages under construction.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** landing page

**RULE:** Landing page must match ad content — the product, offer, or service described in the ad must be immediately accessible on the landing page.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection
**Applies to:** landing page

**RULE:** No deceptive or misleading landing page content — the landing page must not contain hidden redirects, auto-downloads, or content that contradicts the ad.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection; deceptive practices = account disabled
**Applies to:** landing page

## AI Content Disclosure

**RULE:** AI-generated, substantially modified, or composited visual/audio content requires an "AI-generated" label. This includes AI product images, background replacement, face/body modification, and synthetic voiceovers. Meta scans for C2PA metadata and synthetic artifacts. Non-disclosure results in rejection and a policy strike. Exception: Advantage+ Creative system modifications are exempt.
**Consequence:** Ad rejection; policy strike
**Applies to:** image, video

## Enforcement

### Account Health Score (0-100)

| Score Range | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| 50-100 | Normal delivery and review times |
| 25-49 | Increased review times; higher rejection rates |
| 0-24 | "Restricted Delivery" with severely limited impressions |

Recovery: gradual improvement over 90 days of clean operation.

### Top Rejection Categories (2026)

1. Personal Attributes — 24%
2. Misleading Claims — 19%
3. Undisclosed AI Content — 14%
4. HEC Category Evasion — 12%
5. Landing Page Mismatch — 11%

### Proofreader Quick Reference — Copy Red Flags

- Second-person health/financial/personal questions ("Are you...?")
- Specific weight loss amounts or timeframes
- Guaranteed financial returns or income claims
- "Clinically proven" without certification
- Superlative medical claims ("cure", "eliminate", "reverse")
- Transformation language paired with outcome imagery

### Proofreader Quick Reference — Visual Red Flags

- Before/after comparison images (any health/weight/cosmetic context)
- Fake UI elements (play buttons, close buttons, notification badges)
- Undisclosed AI-generated photorealistic content
- Implied transformation sequences (lighting changes, posture shifts)
- QR codes redirecting to unreviewed destinations
